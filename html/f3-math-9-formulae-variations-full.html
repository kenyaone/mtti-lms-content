Formulae — Changing the Subject

To make a variable the subject, isolate it using inverse operations (same as solving equations).

Example 1: Make t the subject of v = u + at
v − u = at → t = (v − u)/a
Example 2: Make r the subject of V = πr²h
r² = V/(πh) → r = √(V/πh)
Example 3: Make b the subject of A = ½h(a + b)
2A = h(a + b) → 2A/h = a + b → b = 2A/h − a
Example 4: Make x the subject of y = (2x + 3)/(x − 1)
y(x − 1) = 2x + 3 → yx − y = 2x + 3
yx − 2x = 3 + y → x(y − 2) = 3 + y → x = (3 + y)/(y − 2)

Direct Variation

y varies directly as x means: as x increases, y increases proportionally.

y ∝ x   →   y = kx
k = y/x = constant of variation
Example 1: y = 15 when x = 3. Find k, then find y when x = 8.
k = y/x = 15/3 = 5  →  y = 5x
When x = 8: y = 5 × 8 = 40
TypeStatementEquation
y ∝ xy varies as xy = kx
y ∝ x²y varies as square of xy = kx²
y ∝ √xy varies as square root of xy = k√x
y ∝ x³y varies as cube of xy = kx³

Inverse Variation

y varies inversely as x: as x increases, y decreases.

y ∝ 1/x   →   y = k/x   →   xy = k
Example: y = 6 when x = 4. Find y when x = 12.
k = xy = 6 × 4 = 24
y = 24/12 = 2
TypeStatementEquation
y ∝ 1/xy varies inversely as xy = k/x
y ∝ 1/x²y inv. varies as x²y = k/x²
y ∝ 1/√xy inv. varies as √xy = k/√x
Example 2: P varies inversely as Q². P=9 when Q=2. Find P when Q=6.
k = PQ² = 9 × 4 = 36  →  P = 36/Q²
P = 36/36 = 1

Joint and Partial Variation

Joint Variation

y varies jointly as x and z: y ∝ xz → y = kxz

Example: V ∝ r²h. V=12π when r=3, h=4/3. Find k, then V when r=2, h=5.
12π = k × 9 × (4/3) = 12k → k = π
V = πr²h (cylinder formula confirmed!)
V = π × 4 × 5 = 20π cm³

Partial Variation

y = ax + b (fixed part b + variable part ax)

Example: y = ax + b. y=11 when x=2, y=23 when x=5.
11 = 2a + b … (i)    23 = 5a + b … (ii)
(ii)−(i): 12 = 3a → a = 4
b = 11 − 8 = 3  →  y = 4x + 3

Key Steps for Variation Problems:
  1. Write the variation statement (∝)
  2. Replace ∝ with = k
  3. Substitute known values to find k
  4. Rewrite formula with k, then find unknown
