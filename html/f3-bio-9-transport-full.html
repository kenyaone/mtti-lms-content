Key Concepts

Overview of Transport in Plants and Animals

Transport in Plants and Animals is a core topic in Form 3 Biology. It involves understanding the biological structures, processes and adaptations related to transport in plants and animals. Study how living organisms carry out these functions and how they are adapted to their environment.

Structure & Function

Biological Structures

For Transport in Plants and Animals, you must be able to: (1) Name and identify relevant structures, (2) Describe the function of each structure, (3) Explain how structure relates to function, (4) Draw and label diagrams accurately.

Diagram Tips

  • Use a sharp pencil and ruler for lines
  • Label all parts clearly with horizontal lines
  • Include a title and magnification if required
  • Show correct proportions
  • Arrows show direction of processes (e.g. blood flow)

Biological Processes

How It Works

Study the step-by-step process for transport in plants and animals from your KLB Biology Book 3. For each process understand: what triggers it, what happens at each stage, what the end products are, and how it is regulated.

Adaptations

Living organisms are adapted for efficient transport in plants and animals. Look for features that: increase surface area, reduce diffusion distance, maintain concentration gradients, provide energy, or regulate the process.

Study tip: For every adaptation, always explain HOW it helps — not just what it is.

Comparisons & Differences

Important Comparisons in Transport in Plants and Animals

Biology exams often ask you to compare two related concepts. For Transport in Plants and Animals, be ready to compare: plants vs animals, different organisms, different processes, or different structures. Use a table format where possible.

How to Answer Comparison Questions

  • State the feature being compared
  • Give the value/description for organism A
  • Give the value/description for organism B
  • Use comparative language: larger/smaller, faster/slower, present/absent

Human Biology Connection

Relevance to Human Health

Transport in Plants and Animals has direct relevance to human health and medicine. Understanding transport in plants and animals helps explain how diseases occur, how the body maintains homeostasis, and how medical treatments work.

Diseases & Disorders

For Transport in Plants and Animals, know the common diseases/disorders related to this system: their causes, symptoms, prevention and treatment. This is frequently tested in KCSE Biology.

Homeostasis: The ability of the body to maintain a stable internal environment despite external changes. Many systems in transport in plants and animals contribute to homeostasis.

Exam Preparation

Common Exam Questions on Transport in Plants and Animals

  • Define [key term] (1-2 marks)

Answer Technique

  • Use correct biological terminology
  • Be precise — vague answers lose marks
  • For processes, use sequence words: first, then, finally
  • Diagrams must be labelled to earn full marks
