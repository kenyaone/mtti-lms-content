🌐 Great Circles and Small Circles

The Earth as a Sphere

When you cut a 'slice' through a sphere, the cross-section is a circle. The type of circle depends on where the cut is made.

Great Circle: A slice through the centre of the Earth. Its radius equals the Earth's radius (≈ 6,371 km). It divides the Earth into two equal halves.

Small Circle: Any slice NOT through the centre. Its radius is smaller than the Earth's radius.

Examples of great circles: The Equator, all meridians of longitude.

Examples of small circles: All parallels of latitude (except the Equator).

Relationship Between Radii

If R is the Earth's radius and a circle is at latitude θ:

r = R × cos θ

At the Equator (θ = 0°): r = R (great circle)
At θ = 60°N: r = R × cos60° = 0.5R (small circle)

📍 Latitude

What is Latitude?

Latitudes are imaginary lines running around the Earth, parallel to the Equator. Their planes are perpendicular to the Earth's axis.

The Equator is at 0° latitude — the only great circle among the latitudes. It divides the Earth into the Northern and Southern hemispheres.

The angle of latitude is measured from the Equator at the centre O of the Earth:

  • Range: 0° (Equator) to 90°N (North Pole) or 90°S (South Pole)
  • Written as: 30°N, 45°S, etc.

Kenyan Context 🇰🇪

Nairobi is at approximately 1.3°S latitude — just south of the Equator.
Eldoret is at approximately 0.52°N — very close to the Equator!

Example: Which is further from the Equator — Mombasa (4°S) or Cairo (30°N)?
Cairo is further: 30° > 4°

🧭 Longitude (Meridians)

What is Longitude?

Longitudes (meridians) are imaginary semicircles running from the North Pole to the South Pole through the Earth.

The Prime Meridian (Greenwich Meridian) is at 0° longitude — it passes through Greenwich, London, England. All other meridians are measured East or West of this line.
  • Range: 0° to 180°E or 180°W
  • All meridians are half great circles
  • They meet at the North and South Poles

Locating a Point on Earth

Any point on Earth can be uniquely identified by its latitude and longitude:

Position = (Latitude, Longitude)
e.g. Nairobi = (1.3°S, 36.8°E)
Kenya's approximate location: Between 4°N–4°S latitude and 34°E–42°E longitude

📏 Distance Calculations

Distance Along a Great Circle (Longitude)

1 nautical mile (nm) = the arc length of 1 minute of latitude on a great circle.

Distance (nm) = difference in degrees × 60
Distance (km) = distance (nm) × 1.852
Example: Find the distance between Nairobi (1°S, 37°E) and Cairo (30°N, 37°E) along their common meridian.
Difference in latitude = 30° + 1° = 31°
Distance = 31 × 60 = 1,860 nm = 1,860 × 1.852 = 3,444.7 km

Distance Along a Small Circle (Latitude)

Distance (nm) = difference in longitude × 60 × cos θ
(where θ is the latitude)
Example: Two cities both at 60°N — A at 20°E and B at 50°E. Find distance.
Difference in longitude = 50° – 20° = 30°
r = R cos 60° = R × 0.5
Distance = 30 × 60 × cos60° = 1800 × 0.5 = 900 nm

⏰ Time and Longitude

The Relationship

The Earth rotates 360° in 24 hours. So it rotates:

360° ÷ 24 hours = 15° per hour
1° of longitude = 4 minutes of time
Places East of Greenwich are ahead in time. Places West are behind.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Nairobi is at 37°E. What is the local time when Greenwich time is 10:00 am?
Time difference = 37 × 4 = 148 minutes = 2 hours 28 minutes
Nairobi time = 10:00 + 2:28 = 12:28 pm
Example 2: New York is at 74°W. What is the local time when Nairobi (37°E) shows 3:00 pm?
Total difference = 37° + 74° = 111° = 111 × 4 = 444 min = 7 hr 24 min
New York = 3:00 pm – 7:24 = 7:36 am

Speed in Knots

1 knot = 1 nautical mile per hour
Speed (knots) = Distance (nm) ÷ Time (hours)

🔢 More Worked Examples

Example: Radius of a Latitude Circle

Find the radius of the circle of latitude 50°N. (Earth's radius R = 6370 km)
r = R × cos 50° = 6370 × 0.6428 = 4,094.7 km

Example: Complete Navigation Problem

A ship sails from A (45°N, 20°W) to B (45°N, 80°W) along the 45°N parallel.
(a) Distance in nm:
Diff in longitude = 80° – 20° = 60°
Distance = 60 × 60 × cos45° = 3600 × 0.7071 = 2,545.6 nm

(b) Distance in km:
= 2545.6 × 1.852 = 4,714.5 km

(c) If speed = 20 knots, time taken:
Time = 2545.6 ÷ 20 = 127.3 hours ≈ 5 days 7.3 hrs

📋 Key Formulas Summary

Radius of small circle: r = R cos θ

Distance along meridian (great circle):
d = Δlat° × 60 nm

Distance along parallel (small circle):
d = Δlong° × 60 × cos θ nm

Time difference:
ΔT = Δlong° × 4 minutes

Speed: Speed (knots) = Distance (nm) / Time (hours)

nm to km: × 1.852

Quick Reminders

  • East = ahead of Greenwich time
  • West = behind Greenwich time
  • Only the Equator is a great circle among latitudes
  • All meridians are great circles (half)
  • 1 nautical mile = 1.852 km
