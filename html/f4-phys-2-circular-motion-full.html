🔄 Introduction to Circular Motion

Circular motion — motion of bodies travelling in circular paths.

Uniform circular motion — constant speed along a curved path of constant radius. Even though speed is constant, velocity changes (direction changes), so acceleration exists.

Centripetal Acceleration & Force

Acceleration directed towards the centre of the circular path = centripetal acceleration.

The force producing this acceleration = centripetal force (also directed towards the centre).

📐 The Radian & Angular Velocity

The Radian

One radian = angle subtended at the centre by an arc equal to the radius.

360° = 2π radians  →  1 radian ≈ 57.3°

Angular Velocity (ω)

Rate of change of angle with time:

ω = θ/t    (unit: rad s⁻¹)

Relating ω to Linear Velocity v

v = rω    (r = radius)

Angular Acceleration (α)

α = (ω₂ − ω₁)/t    a = rα
📌 In uniform circular motion, α = 0 (ω is constant).

Example

A wheel (r = 50 cm) rotates through a quarter turn. Find: (i) angle in radians, (ii) arc length.

(i) 90° = π/2 = 1.57 rad

(ii) arc = r × θ = 0.5 × 1.57 = 0.785 m

💪 Centripetal Force

A force acting towards the centre directing the body in circular motion:

Fc = mv²/r = mRω²
centripetal acceleration: a = v²/r = Rω²

Motion in a Vertical Circle

A mass m on a string of length r in a vertical circle:

PositionTension equationNotes
Top (position 1)T₁ + mg = mv²/r → T₁ = mv²/r − mgMinimum speed: v = √(rg) when T₁ = 0
Side (position 2)T₂ = mv²/rmg has no centripetal component
Bottom (position 3)T₃ − mg = mv²/r → T₃ = mv²/r + mgGreatest tension

Example 1

Ball (m = 0.025 kg) in horizontal circle, v = 5 m/s, r = 2 m. Find Fc.

Fc = mv²/r = 0.025 × 25 / 2 = 0.31 N

Example 2

Stone on 80 cm string in vertical circle. Find minimum speed at top.

v = √(rg) = √(0.8 × 10) = 2.83 m/s

🔺 Conical Pendulum & Banked Tracks

Conical Pendulum

Object on a string whirled in a horizontal circle. The string makes angle θ with the vertical.

When speed increases → θ increases → radius r increases → object rises.

T cos θ = mg (vertical equilibrium)

T sin θ = mv²/r (centripetal force)

Banked Tracks

Road raised on outer edge (saucer shape) — this is banking. Part of the centripetal force is provided by the vehicle's weight component, allowing cars to negotiate bends at higher speeds without skidding.

Centripetal force = mv²/r = frictional force (unbanked)

The Centrifuge

Rotating tubes separate particles by density — heavier particles need greater centripetal force → move to outer radius (bottom of tube). Used to separate cream from milk, plasma from blood.

✏️ More Worked Examples

Example 1

A car (m = 6000 kg) on a 250 m radius curve. Friction = 21,000 N. Find max speed.

Fc = mv²/r → 21000 = 6000 × v²/250

v² = 21000 × 250/6000 = 875 → v = 29.6 m/s

Example 2

An object moves in a circle of radius 0.5 m at ω = 4 rad/s. Find (a) v (b) Fc if m = 2 kg.

(a) v = rω = 0.5 × 4 = 2 m/s

(b) Fc = mRω² = 2 × 0.5 × 16 = 16 N

Example 3

A wheel rotates at 300 rpm. Find (a) angular velocity (b) linear velocity of a point 0.3 m from centre.

(a) ω = 2π × 300/60 = 10π ≈ 31.4 rad/s

(b) v = rω = 0.3 × 31.4 = 9.42 m/s

🧪 Practice Problems

Problem 1

A satellite orbits Earth at 400 km altitude with speed 7.67 km/s. The radius of Earth = 6400 km. Find the orbital period in minutes.

r = 6400 + 400 = 6800 km = 6.8×10⁶ m
T = 2πr/v = 2π×6.8×10⁶/(7670) = 5566 s ≈ 92.8 min

Problem 2

A stone of mass 0.2 kg is whirled in a vertical circle of radius 1.5 m at minimum speed at the top. Find: (a) minimum speed, (b) tension at the bottom if speed is maintained.

(a) v_min = √(rg) = √(1.5×10) = 3.87 m/s
(b) At bottom: T₃ = mv²/r + mg = 0.2×15/1.5 + 0.2×10 = 2+2 = 4 N
