Preparation and Properties of Chlorine

Laboratory Preparation

Chlorine is prepared by oxidising concentrated hydrochloric acid with manganese(IV) oxide (MnO2) on warming.

MnO2 + 4HCl(conc.) rarr MnCl2 + 2H2O + Cl2 (gas, warm)

Alternatively: electrolysis of brine (NaCl solution) - industrial method.

Chlorine is a poisonous, yellow-green gas with a pungent smell. Always use in a fume cupboard.

Physical Properties

  • Yellow-green gas at room temperature
  • Pungent, choking smell
  • Denser than air (relative molecular mass = 71)
  • Slightly soluble in water to form chlorine water
  • Melting point: -101 degrees C; Boiling point: -35 degrees C

Test for Chlorine Gas

Moist blue litmus paper turns red then is bleached white. (Cl2 dissolves in water to form HClO which bleaches.)

Chemical Reactions of Chlorine

Reaction with Water (Disproportionation)

Cl2 + H2O rarr HCl + HClO (hypochlorous acid)

HClO is a weak acid and is responsible for bleaching action. In sunlight, HClO decomposes:

2HClO rarr 2HCl + O2 (nascent oxygen - bleaches)

Reaction with Alkalis

With cold dilute NaOH (bleach):
Cl2 + 2NaOH rarr NaCl + NaClO + H2O (sodium hypochlorite - Jik/bleach)
With hot concentrated NaOH:
3Cl2 + 6NaOH rarr 5NaCl + NaClO3 + 3H2O (sodium chlorate(V))
With slaked lime Ca(OH)2 (bleaching powder):
2Cl2 + 2Ca(OH)2 rarr Ca(ClO)2 + CaCl2 + 2H2O

Reaction with Metals

  • 2Fe + 3Cl2 rarr 2FeCl3 (iron(III) chloride - brown fumes)
  • 2Na + Cl2 rarr 2NaCl (vigorous)
  • Cu + Cl2 rarr CuCl2 (copper(II) chloride)

Reaction with Hydrogen (synthesis of HCl)

H2 + Cl2 rarr 2HCl (burns with pale blue flame, explosive mixture in sunlight)
Hydrogen Chloride and Hydrochloric Acid

Preparation of HCl Gas

NaCl (solid) + conc. H2SO4 rarr NaHSO4 + HCl (gas) on gentle warming

HCl gas is dense, colourless, acidic. Fumes in moist air. Collected by downward delivery or dissolved in water.

Properties of HCl Gas

  • Colourless gas with pungent smell
  • Denser than air
  • Highly soluble in water - dissolves with hissing sound (fountain experiment)
  • NOT an acid in the dry state (requires water to ionise)
HCl(g) is molecular (not acidic in dry state). When dissolved: HCl(aq) rarr H+(aq) + Cl-(aq) - now a strong acid.

Reactions of Hydrochloric Acid

  • With metals: Mg + 2HCl rarr MgCl2 + H2 (effervescence)
  • With carbonates: CaCO3 + 2HCl rarr CaCl2 + H2O + CO2
  • With bases: NaOH + HCl rarr NaCl + H2O
  • With silver nitrate: HCl + AgNO3 rarr AgCl (white ppt) + HNO3 (test for Cl- ions)
Chlorine Compounds and Uses

Bleaching Powder (Calcium Hypochlorite)

Made by passing Cl2 over slaked lime (Ca(OH)2). Formula: Ca(ClO)2.CaCl2 (mixture).

  • Used for bleaching cotton and linen
  • Disinfecting water supplies (swimming pools, drinking water)
  • Bleaching paper pulp
Bleaching powder releases Cl2 with dilute acids, which then dissolves in water to form HClO (the active bleaching agent).

Sodium Hypochlorite (NaClO) - Household Bleach

  • Made by: Cl2 + 2NaOH rarr NaCl + NaClO + H2O
  • Active ingredient in Jik, Clorox, swimming pool treatment

Halogens - Trends and Comparisons

The Halogens (Group VII)

HalogenSymbolState (R.T.)ColourReactivity
FluorineF2GasPale yellowHighest
ChlorineCl2GasYellow-greenHigh
BromineBr2LiquidBrown/redMedium
IodineI2SolidGrey/violet vapourLow

Reactivity decreases down the group (harder to gain an electron as atomic radius increases).

Displacement Reactions of Halogens

A more reactive halogen displaces a less reactive halogen from its salt solution.

Cl2 + 2KBr rarr 2KCl + Br2 (chlorine displaces bromine - brown colour)
Cl2 + 2KI rarr 2KCl + I2 (chlorine displaces iodine - brown/violet colour)
Br2 + 2KI rarr 2KBr + I2 (bromine displaces iodine)

Note: Iodine cannot displace bromine or chlorine (less reactive).

Environmental and Health Hazards

Hazards of Chlorine

  • Toxic - used as a chemical weapon in World War I
  • Damages the respiratory system: causes choking, pulmonary oedema
  • Exposure limit: 1 ppm (OSHA) in air
  • Chlorine in water reacts with organic matter to form trihalomethanes (THMs) - potential carcinogens

Hazards of Chlorine Compounds

  • CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons): Destroy the ozone layer (Cl atoms catalyse ozone breakdown)
  • DDT: Persistent organic pollutant - bioaccumulates in food chains; banned in many countries
  • Dioxins: Toxic by-products from burning chlorinated plastics (PVC)
  • HCl emissions: From industrial processes - contributes to acid rain

Safety in Handling Chlorine

  • Use in fume cupboard only
  • Wear goggles and gloves
  • Keep quantities minimal
  • Store bleach/HCl in sealed containers, away from heat
