Length of an Arc

An arc is a portion of the circumference. Its length depends on the central angle θ and radius r.

Arc length = (θ/360) × 2πr
Area of sector = (θ/360) × πr²
Example 1: Arc for θ = 250°, r = 14 cm (π = 22/7)
Arc = (250/360) × 2 × (22/7) × 14 = (250/360) × 88 = 61.11 cm
Example 2: Arc = 11 cm, θ = 90°. Find r.
11 = (90/360) × 2πr = (1/4) × 2πr = πr/2
r = 22/π = 22/(22/7) = 7 cm
 

Chords

A chord is a line segment joining two points on a circle. Key properties:

  1. The perpendicular from the centre bisects the chord
  2. Equal chords are equidistant from the centre
  3. A chord closer to the centre is longer
If d = perpendicular distance from centre, half-chord = √(r² − d²)
Chord length = 2√(r² − d²)
Example: Circle radius 13 cm, chord 10 cm from centre. Find chord length.
Half-chord = √(13² − 10²) = √(169 − 100) = √69 ≈ 8.307 cm
Full chord = 2 × 8.307 = 16.61 cm
Example: Chord = 24 cm, radius = 13 cm. Find distance from centre.
Half-chord = 12; d = √(13² − 12²) = √(169 − 144) = √25 = 5 cm

Tangent of a Circle

A tangent touches the circle at exactly one point. Key properties:

  1. A tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact
  2. From an external point, two equal tangents can be drawn
  3. Tangent-chord angle = angle in alternate segment
Tangent from external point P: PT² = PA × PB (where A, B are intersection of secant)
PT² = PO² − r²   (PT = tangent length)
Example: O is centre, r = 5 cm, OP = 13 cm. Find tangent length PT.
PT² = OP² − r² = 169 − 25 = 144
PT = 12 cm

Alternate Segment Theorem

The angle between a tangent to a circle and a chord drawn from the point of contact equals the inscribed angle on the opposite side of the chord.

∠(tangent, chord) = ∠ in alternate segment
Example: Tangent PT at A. Chord AB. ∠TAB = 55°. Find ∠ACB (C in alternate segment).
By alternate segment theorem: ∠ACB = ∠TAB = 55°

Cyclic Quadrilateral Properties

  • Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary (add to 180°)
  • Exterior angle = opposite interior angle
Example: ABCD is cyclic. ∠A = 75°. Find ∠C.
∠A + ∠C = 180° → ∠C = 180° − 75° = 105°
