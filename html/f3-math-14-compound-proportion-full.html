Proportions

Four numbers a, b, c, d are in proportion if a/b = c/d, i.e., a:b = c:d.

a/b = c/d   →   ad = bc (cross-multiply)
Example 1: Find a if 4, 10, a, 50 are in proportion.
4/10 = a/50 → 10a = 200 → a = 20

Continued Proportion

a, b, c are in continued proportion if a:b = b:c, i.e., b² = ac.

Example 2: Find b if 4, b, 9 are in continued proportion.
b² = 4×9 = 36 → b = 6

Compound Proportion

When a quantity depends on more than one other quantity simultaneously.

Example: 5 workers take 12 days to build a wall. How long for 8 workers?
More workers → fewer days (inverse proportion)
5 × 12 = 8 × d → d = 60/8 = 7.5 days
Example 2 (compound): 6 men working 8 hrs/day take 5 days. How many days for 4 men working 10 hrs/day?
Total worker-hours = 6 × 8 × 5 = 240
New: 4 × 10 × d = 240 → d = 240/40 = 6 days

Proportional Parts

Divide a quantity in a given ratio.

Each part = (ratio share / sum of ratio) × total
Example: Divide Ksh 12 000 among A, B, C in ratio 2:3:5.
Sum = 2+3+5 = 10
A = (2/10)×12000 = Ksh 2 400
B = (3/10)×12000 = Ksh 3 600
C = (5/10)×12000 = Ksh 6 000

Rates of Work

Rate of work = fraction of task completed per unit time.

If a person completes a job in n days → rate = 1/n per day
Combined rate = sum of individual rates
Example: A takes 6 days, B takes 4 days. Working together, how long?
Combined rate = 1/6 + 1/4 = 2/12 + 3/12 = 5/12 per day
Time = 12/5 = 2.4 days
Example 2: Pipe A fills in 3 hrs, pipe B fills in 6 hrs. Both open. How long to fill?
Rate = 1/3 + 1/6 = 2/6 + 1/6 = 3/6 = ½ per hr → 2 hours

Mixtures

When two quantities of different concentrations or values are mixed.

Example: Tea at Ksh 80/kg mixed with tea at Ksh 120/kg in ratio 3:2 to get 50 kg mixture. Find quantities and cost.
Quantity at 80: (3/5)×50 = 30 kg; Cost = 30×80 = Ksh 2 400
Quantity at 120: (2/5)×50 = 20 kg; Cost = 20×120 = Ksh 2 400
Total cost = Ksh 4 800; Price per kg = 4800/50 = Ksh 96/kg
