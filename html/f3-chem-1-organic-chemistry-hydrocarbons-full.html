What is Organic Chemistry?

Organic chemistry is the study of carbon compounds except the oxides of carbon (CO, CO₂) and carbonates. It focuses on the chemistry of hydrogen-carbon chain compounds.

Hydrocarbons are compounds containing hydrogen and carbon only — the simplest organic compounds.

Alkanes – Saturated Hydrocarbons

Definition

Alkanes are hydrocarbons in which all carbon atoms are linked by single covalent bonds. General formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂

They are saturated — they do not decolourise bromine water or acidified KMnO₄.

Chemical Properties of Methane

Combustion (burning):

CH₄(g) + 2O₂(g) → CO₂(g) + 2H₂O(g)

Substitution with Bromine (UV light):

CH₄(g) + Br₂(g) → CH₃Br(g) + HBr(g)

Cracking of Alkanes — breaking large molecules into smaller alkanes and alkenes at 400–700°C.

Lab preparation of methane:

CH₃COONa(s) + NaOH(s) → CH₄(g) + Na₂CO₃(aq)

Alkenes – Unsaturated Hydrocarbons

Definition

Alkenes contain at least one carbon–carbon double bond. General formula: CₙH₂ₙ

They are unsaturated — they decolourise bromine water and acidified KMnO₄ by addition reactions at the double bond.

Lab Preparation of Ethene

Ethanol + concentrated H₂SO₄ heated to 160–180°C (dehydration):

C₂H₅OH(l) → C₂H₄(g) + H₂O(l) [conc. H₂SO₄, 160–180°C]

Alkynes – Highly Unsaturated Hydrocarbons

Definition

Alkynes contain at least one carbon–carbon triple bond. General formula: CₙH₂ₙ₋₂

Functional group: –C≡C– (triple bond)

Lab Preparation of Ethyne

Calcium carbide + water at room temperature:

CaC₂(s) + 2H₂O(l) → C₂H₂(g) + Ca(OH)₂(aq)

Collected over water (insoluble, lighter than water).

Reactions of Ethyne

Combustion: Burns with a luminous sooty flame (high C content).

2C₂H₂(g) + 5O₂(g) → 4CO₂(g) + 2H₂O(g)

Addition with Br₂: Decolourises bromine water (two stages — triple bond breaks).

C₂H₂ + Br₂ → CHBr=CHBr → CHBr₂–CHBr₂ (1,1,2,2-tetrabromoethane)

Addition with H₂ (hydrogenation): Ni catalyst, ~200°C → ethene then ethane.

Test for alkynes: Decolourise bromine water and acidified KMnO₄ (like alkenes).

Uses of ethyne: Oxy-acetylene welding and cutting metals; manufacture of adhesives and plastics.

Homologous Series, Isomerism & Nomenclature

Homologous Series

A group of organic compounds that:

  • Have the same general formula
  • Consecutive members differ by –CH₂–
  • Have similar chemical properties
  • Show a steady gradation of physical properties

Isomerism

Two or more compounds with the same molecular formula but different structural formulae.

Example: Butane (C₄H₁₀) has two structural isomers:

  • n-butane: CH₃CH₂CH₂CH₃
  • 2-methylpropane: (CH₃)₃CH

Alkenes show: branching isomerism and positional isomerism (position of double bond).

Naming Rules (IUPAC Nomenclature)

Alkanes: Suffix -ane. For branched chains:

  1. Find longest carbon chain (parent name)
  2. Number carbons so branches get lowest numbers
  3. Name substituents (methyl, ethyl, etc.) as prefixes

Alkenes: Suffix -ene. Double bond position stated (e.g. but-2-ene).

Alkynes: Suffix -yne. Triple bond position stated (e.g. pent-2-yne).

Alkyl groups: Alkane minus one H → methyl (–CH₃), ethyl (–C₂H₅), propyl (–C₃H₇)

Alkane combustion:

CₙH₂ₙ₊₂ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O

Alkane substitution (halogenation):

CH₄ + Br₂ → CH₃Br + HBr [UV light]

CH₂=CH₂ + H₂ → CH₃–CH₃ [Ni, ~200°C]

Polymerisation:

nCH₂=CH₂ → –(CH₂–CH₂)ₙ– (polythene)

Crude Oil, Natural Gas & Environmental Context

Natural Sources of Alkanes

  • Natural gas: Mainly methane (CH₄)
  • Crude oil: Mixture of many alkanes; separated by fractional distillation
  • Biogas: 60–75% methane (from decomposition of organic matter)

Gradation of Physical Properties in Alkane Series

As the number of carbon atoms increases in the alkane homologous series:

  • Boiling point increases (stronger van der Waals forces)
  • Viscosity increases
  • Colour intensity increases
  • Flammability decreases
  • State changes from gas → liquid → solid
⚠️ A mixture of methane and air (ratio ~1:10) is explosive when ignited — the cause of coal mine explosions.

Cracking

Cracking breaks large alkane molecules into smaller, more useful molecules including alkenes:

C₁₀H₂₂ → C₄H₈ + C₃H₆ + CH₄ + H₂ [400–700°C, catalyst]

Cracking is important industrially to produce petrol and alkenes for polymer manufacture.

Complete Combustion vs Incomplete Combustion

Complete combustion (excess O₂): CO₂ + H₂O, pale blue flame

C₄H₁₀ + 6½O₂ → 4CO₂ + 5H₂O

Incomplete combustion (limited O₂): CO + C (soot) + H₂O, luminous sooty flame

💡 A sooty flame when a hydrocarbon burns is an indication of unsaturation or high carbon content.

All Key Reactions at a Glance

Methane + NaOH (lab prep):

CH₃COONa + NaOH → CH₄ + Na₂CO₃ [heat]

Ethene + H₂SO₄ (industrial ethanol):

CH₂=CH₂ + H₂SO₄ → CH₃CH₂HSO₄ → CH₃CH₂OH [hydrolysis]

Ethyne + CaC₂:

CaC₂ + 2H₂O → HC≡CH + Ca(OH)₂

Butene + Br₂:

CH₃CH=CHCH₃ + Br₂ → CH₃CHBr–CHBrCH₃

Q5. Explain what is meant by a homologous series and give two characteristics.
A homologous series is a group of organic compounds with the same general formula, where consecutive members differ by a –CH₂– unit. They have similar chemical properties and show a steady gradation of physical properties.
Q6. Describe the test for an unsaturated hydrocarbon and state the expected result.
Add bromine water to the compound. If the compound is unsaturated (alkene or alkyne), the red-brown bromine water is decolourised. Alternatively, acidified potassium manganate(VII) can be used — the purple colour turns colourless with unsaturated compounds.
