⚡ Production of Cathode Rays

Cathode rays — streams of electrons emitted at the cathode of an evacuated tube. Discovered by J.J. Thomson in the 18th century.

Discharge Tube

EHT (Extra High Tension) supply across tube with air at low pressure → luminous electron discharge between brass rods → cathode rays.

SLIDE 3 OF 10

🔬 Properties of Cathode Rays

  1. Travel in straight lines
  2. Particulate — negatively charged electrons
  3. Deflected by both electric and magnetic fields (towards positive plates)
  4. Cause fluorescence in some materials
  5. Can penetrate thin sheets of paper and metal foils
  6. When stopped, produce X-rays
  7. Affect photographic plates
SLIDE 4 OF 10

📺 Cathode Ray Oscilloscope (CRO)

Components

  • Cathode Ray Tube (CRT) — tube, electron gun, deflection plates, time base (TB)
  • Electron gun — filament, cathode, grid, anode
  • Y-deflection plates — deflect beam up/down (voltage measurement)
  • X-deflection plates — sweep beam left/right (time base)
  • Fluorescent screen — shows waveform

Grid controls brightness. Time base produces a waveform from AC input.

SLIDE 5 OF 10

📏 CRO Measurements

Measuring Frequency

Period T = wavelength (cm) × time base (s/cm)

f = 1/T

Example

Time base = 10 ms/cm, wavelength = 1.8 cm

T = 1.8 × 10 ms = 18 ms = 0.018 s

f = 1/0.018 = 56 Hz

Television (TV)

A CRT with both Y and X deflection plates. Screen coated with phosphor dots (chemicals) that produce different colours when struck by electron beam.

SLIDE 6 OF 10

🔍 The Electron Gun in Detail

  • Filament — heats the cathode
  • Cathode — emits electrons (thermionic emission)
  • Grid — controls number of electrons → controls brightness
  • Anode — accelerates electrons towards screen

Electrons travel through vacuum in the tube as a focused beam.

SLIDE 8 OF 10

✏️ More Examples

Example 1

CRO time base: 5 ms/cm. One complete wave spans 4 cm. Find frequency.

T = 4 × 5 ms = 20 ms = 0.02 s | f = 1/0.02 = 50 Hz

Example 2

CRO Y-sensitivity: 2 V/cm. A waveform has amplitude of 3 cm. Find peak voltage.

Vpeak = 3 × 2 = 6 V
