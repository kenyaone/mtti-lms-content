Surds

Form Three Mathematics — KLB Book 3

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Define surds and identify rational and irrational numbers
  • Simplify surds by removing perfect square factors
  • Add, subtract, multiply and divide surds
  • Rationalise the denominator of surd expressions
  • Solve equations involving surds

What are Surds?

Definition

A surd is an irrational number that cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers. It is a root that cannot be simplified to remove the radical sign.

✅ Surds

  • √2 = 1.41421...
  • √3 = 1.73205...
  • √5, √7, √11
  • ∛2, ∛5
  • 2 + √3

❌ Not Surds (Rational)

  • √4 = 2
  • √9 = 3
  • √(4/9) = 2/3
  • ∛8 = 2
  • √0.25 = 0.5
Note: A surd is always irrational, but not all irrational numbers are surds (e.g. π, e are irrational but not surds).

Simplifying Surds

Rule: √(ab) = √a × √b

Find the largest perfect square factor, then simplify.

Adding & Subtracting Surds

Rule: Only like surds can be added/subtracted

Just like algebra: 3x + 5x = 8x, similarly 3√2 + 5√2 = 8√2

Examples

1. 3√5 + 7√5 = 10√5

2. 5√3 − 2√3 = 3√3

3. √8 + √18 = 2√2 + 3√2 = 5√2

4. √12 + √27 = 2√3 + 3√3 = 5√3

5. √50 − √32 = 5√2 − 4√2 = √2

Tip: Always simplify surds first before adding or subtracting. √8 and √18 look different but both simplify to multiples of √2.

Multiplying & Dividing Surds

Rules

√a × √b = √(ab)  |  √a ÷ √b = √(a/b)

Multiplication Examples

1. √3 × √12 = √36 = 6

2. 2√5 × 3√5 = 6 × 5 = 30

3. (2+√3)(2−√3) = 4 − 3 = 1 (difference of squares)

4. (√5 + √2)² = 5 + 2√10 + 2 = 7 + 2√10

Division Examples

1. √20 ÷ √5 = √(20/5) = √4 = 2

2. 6√15 ÷ 2√3 = 3√5

3. √18/√2 = √9 = 3

Rationalising the Denominator

Why Rationalise?

We don't leave surds in the denominator. Rationalising means removing the surd from the bottom of a fraction.

Case 1: Single Surd in Denominator

Multiply top and bottom by the surd:

5/√3 = (5 × √3)/(√3 × √3) = 5√3/3

2/√5 = 2√5/5

6/√2 = 6√2/2 = 3√2

Case 2: Binomial Denominator — Conjugate

Multiply by the conjugate: (a+√b) × (a−√b) = a²−b

1/(2+√3) × (2−√3)/(2−√3)

= (2−√3)/(4−3)

= 2−√3

Equations Involving Surds

Steps to Solve

  1. Isolate the surd on one side
  2. Square both sides to eliminate the surd
  3. Solve the resulting equation
  4. Check solutions in original equation (squaring can introduce extraneous roots)

Examples

1. √x = 5 → x = 25 ✓

2. √(x+3) = 4 → x+3 = 16 → x = 13 ✓

3. √(2x−1) = x−2

Squaring: 2x−1 = x²−4x+4

x²−6x+5 = 0 → (x−1)(x−5) = 0

x=1: √(2−1)=1, 1−2=−1 ✗ (reject)

x=5: √(10−1)=3, 5−2=3 ✓ → x=5
