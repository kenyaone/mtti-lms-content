🔧 Formulae — Change of Subject

What is a Formula?

A formula is a mathematical rule that shows the relationship between two or more quantities using symbols.

Subject of a Formula: The variable on its own on one side of the equation. E.g. in A = πr², A is the subject.

Changing the Subject

We rearrange a formula to make a different variable the subject using inverse operations.

Example 1: Make r the subject of A = πr²

A = πr²

A/π = r²

r = √(A/π)

Example 2: Make u the subject of v = u + at

v = u + at

u = v − at

Example 3: Make h the subject of V = ⅓πr²h

3V = πr²h

h = 3V / πr²

🔢 Substitution into Formulae

Using Formulae

Substitute known values into a formula to find the unknown quantity.

Example 1: Using v² = u² + 2as

Find v when u = 5 m/s, a = 2 m/s², s = 10 m

v² = 5² + 2(2)(10) = 25 + 40 = 65

v = √65 ≈ 8.06 m/s

Example 2: Using A = P(1 + r/100)ⁿ

Find A when P = 10,000, r = 5, n = 3

A = 10,000(1 + 5/100)³ = 10,000(1.05)³

A = 10,000 × 1.1576 = Ksh 11,576.25

📈 Direct Variation

Definition

y varies directly as x means when x increases, y increases proportionally.

y ∝ x   ⟹   y = kx

where k is the constant of variation (k ≠ 0)

To find k: k = y/x (use any known pair of values)

Worked Examples

Example 1:

y varies directly as x. When x = 4, y = 12. Find y when x = 7.

k = y/x = 12/4 = 3

y = 3x = 3 × 7 = 21

Example 2: Direct variation with powers

y ∝ x². When x = 3, y = 45. Find y when x = 5.

k = y/x² = 45/9 = 5

y = 5x² = 5 × 25 = 125

📉 Inverse Variation

Definition

y varies inversely as x means when x increases, y decreases proportionally.

y ∝ 1/x   ⟹   y = k/x   ⟹   xy = k

The product xy is always constant

Worked Examples

Example 1:

y varies inversely as x. When x = 4, y = 6. Find y when x = 12.

k = xy = 4 × 6 = 24

y = k/x = 24/12 = 2

Example 2: Inverse with square

y ∝ 1/x². When x = 2, y = 20. Find y when x = 4.

k = yx² = 20 × 4 = 80

y = 80/x² = 80/16 = 5

🔗 Joint & Partial Variation

Joint Variation

A quantity varies with respect to two or more other quantities.

z ∝ xy   ⟹   z = kxy

z varies jointly as x and y

Example:

z varies jointly as x and y. When x = 2 and y = 3, z = 24. Find z when x = 4, y = 5.

k = z/(xy) = 24/(2×3) = 4

z = 4xy = 4 × 4 × 5 = 80

Partial Variation

y partly varies with x and is partly constant.

y = kx + c

where k and c are constants to be determined

Example:

y partly varies as x and is partly constant. When x = 2, y = 8. When x = 5, y = 17.

8 = 2k + c ... (i)

17 = 5k + c ... (ii)

(ii) − (i): 9 = 3k → k = 3

From (i): c = 8 − 6 = 2

Formula: y = 3x + 2

Worked Example — Square Root Variation

Example:

y varies as the square root of x. When x = 16, y = 12. Find y when x = 25.

y = k√x

12 = k√16 = 4k → k = 3

y = 3√25 = 3 × 5 = 15

Real-Life Application

Stopping distance of a car: The stopping distance d varies directly as the square of the speed v. This means doubling the speed quadruples the stopping distance — a critical road safety fact!

✏️ Practice Problems

Problem 1 — Change of Subject

Make t the subject of the formula: s = ut + ½at²

Solution:

Rearrange: ½at² + ut − s = 0

Using quadratic formula: t = [−u ± √(u² + 2as)] / a

(Taking positive root for physical situations)

Problem 2 — Direct Variation

The cost C of printing varies directly as the number of pages n. 500 pages cost Ksh 250. Find the cost of printing 1,200 pages.

Solution:

k = C/n = 250/500 = 0.5

C = 0.5 × 1200 = Ksh 600

Problem 3 — Inverse Variation

The time t taken to complete a job varies inversely as the number of workers w. 6 workers complete it in 8 days. How long will 12 workers take?

Solution:

k = tw = 8 × 6 = 48

t = 48/12 = 4 days
