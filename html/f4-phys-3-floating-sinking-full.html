🌊 Upthrust & Archimedes' Principle

Upthrust (buoyant force, U) — upward force exerted on an object fully or partially submerged in a fluid.

Archimedes' Principle

"When a body is wholly or partially immersed in a fluid, it experiences an upthrust equal to the weight of the displaced fluid."

Upthrust U = weight of displaced fluid = Vρg

Experimental Verification

  • Weigh object in air → W₁
  • Weigh in water → W₂ (apparent weight)
  • Collect displaced water → W₄ − W₃
  • Result: Upthrust (W₁−W₂) = weight of displaced water (W₄−W₃) ✓

🛟 Law of Floatation

Floating Condition

Objects float when they are less dense than the liquid they are in.

Law of Floatation: A floating body displaces its own weight of liquid.

Mathematically: W = vd × ρ × g

Example

A wooden block (3cm×3cm×4cm) floats in methylated spirit (ρ=800 kg/m³) with 4cm submerged. Find its weight.

Vd = 3×3×4 = 36 cm³ = 3.6×10⁻⁵ m³

W = 3.6×10⁻⁵ × 800 × 10 = 0.288 N

📌 Floatation is a special case of Archimedes' Principle — a floating body sinks until upthrust equals weight.

⚖️ Relative Density

Relative density (d) = density of substance / density of water
d = W / U = W / (W₂ − W₁)

Where W = weight in air, W₁ = weight when fully submerged

Actual density ρ = d × 1000 kg/m³

Example

A solid (m=1.0 kg) is submerged in water with tension T=5 N. Find relative density.

W = 10 N, Upthrust = 10−5 = 5 N

d = W/U = 10/5 = 2

🚢 Applications of Archimedes' Principle

ApplicationPrinciple
ShipsSteel (6-7× denser than water) shaped to displace more water than its own volume. Plimsoll marks show max load.
SubmarinesBallast tanks filled with water to sink, air to rise — balancing weight and upthrust.
BalloonsHelium-filled → lighter than air → upthrust > weight → rises.
HydrometersMeasure relative density of liquids (e.g. lactometer for milk: range 1.015–1.045; battery acid tester).

✏️ Worked Examples

Example 1

A metal block (V=60 cm³) weighs 4.80 N in air. Find its weight in liquid of ρ=1200 kg/m³.

Vd = 6.0×10⁻⁵ m³ | U = 6.0×10⁻⁵×1200×10 = 0.72 N

W in liquid = 4.80 − 0.72 = 4.08 N

Example 2 — Balloon

Fabric (80 N), V = 1.0×10⁷ cm³, hydrogen (ρ=0.09 kg/m³), air ρ=1.25 kg/m³. Find max extra load.

U = 10 × 1.25 × 10 = 125 N

WH₂ = 10 × 0.09 × 10 = 9 N

Extra load = 125 − 80 − 9 = 36 N

🔬 Relative Density of a Floating Body

Experiment — Cork with Sinker

MeasurementSymbol
Sinker in waterW₁
Sinker in water + cork in airW₂
Both in waterW₃

Weight of cork in air = W₂ − W₁

Upthrust on cork = W₂ − W₃

d = (W₂ − W₁) / (W₂ − W₃)

SLIDE 9 OF 10

🧪 Practice Problems

Problem 1

An object weighs 6 N in air and 2 N in water. Find: (a) upthrust, (b) relative density.

(a) U = 6−2 = 4 N
(b) d = 6/4 = 1.5

Problem 2

A piece of wood (ρ=0.6 g/cm³) has mass 300 g. What fraction floats above water surface?

Fraction submerged = ρwood/ρwater = 0.6
Fraction above = 1−0.6 = 0.4 (40%)
