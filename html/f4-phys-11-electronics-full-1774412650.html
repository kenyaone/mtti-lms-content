⚡ Electronic Band Structure

Energy Bands

  • Valence band — highest energy electrons at 0K
  • Conduction band — electrons free to move; responsible for charge transfer
  • Band gap — forbidden zone between valence and conduction bands

InsulatorLargeNo conduction

Intrinsic semiconductors: conductivity increases with temperature (unlike metals).

🔬 Doping Semiconductors

Doping — adding impurities to semiconductors to change conductivity. Can increase conductivity by >10⁶×.

N-type Semiconductor

Donor impurity (e.g. phosphorus/antimony in silicon) contributes extra free electrons. Majority carriers: electrons.

P-type Semiconductor

Acceptor impurity (e.g. boron in silicon) creates holes (missing electrons). Majority carriers: holes.

SLIDE 5 OF 10

⚡ P-N Junction Diode

P-type + N-type semiconductor joined together. P-region = anode, N-region = cathode. A depletion layer (region with no charge carriers) forms at the junction.

Forward Bias

+terminal to P, −terminal to N → depletion layer thins → current flows.

Reverse Bias

−terminal to P, +terminal to N → depletion layer widens → no current (until breakdown voltage).

📌 Diodes allow current in one direction only — from anode (+) to cathode (−).
SLIDE 6 OF 10

🔄 Rectification

Rectification — converting AC to unidirectional DC waveform.

Half-wave Rectification

One diode blocks negative half-cycle → pulsating DC. Smoothed by capacitor in parallel with load.

Full-wave Rectification

  • Centre-tap — transformer with centre tap + 2 diodes
  • Bridge rectifier — 4 diodes in bridge arrangement; current flows in same direction in both half-cycles
SLIDE 7 OF 10

📻 Radio Transmission

Amplitude Modulation (AM)

  1. Sound converted to electrical signal by microphone
  2. Signal added to radio carrier wave → amplitude modulation
  3. Modulated wave transmitted by aerial
  4. Received by another aerial
  5. Demodulated by diode → reproduced as sound

Radio waves: greater range than sound/light → used as carriers for audio and visual information.

🧪 Practice Problems

P1. In silicon doped with phosphorus, what type of semiconductor is formed and what are the majority carriers?

N-type semiconductor. Majority carriers: electrons (donated by phosphorus).

P2. Why does a semiconductor increase conductivity with temperature while a metal decreases?

In semiconductors, higher temperature promotes more electrons across the small band gap into the conduction band. In metals, higher temperature increases lattice vibrations, impeding electron flow.
