⚡ Sources & Transmission

Mains electricity: alternating current (AC) from power stations. AC produced by electromagnetic induction. In East Africa, most electricity generated from water (hydroelectric power).

Power Transmission (National Grid)

  • Generated at ~25 kV at power station
  • Stepped up to 400 kV for long-distance transmission (reduces current → less power loss I²R)
  • Overhead aluminium cables (lighter than copper)
  • Stepped down at sub-stations: 132 kV → 33 kV → 11 kV → 240 V (domestic)
SLIDE 3 OF 10

⚠️ Dangers & Electrical Power

Dangers of High Voltage Transmission

  • Death by electrocution
  • Fires during upsurge
  • EM radiation from power lines — cancer risk

Electrical Power & Energy

P = VI = I²R = V²/R    (watts)
Energy = P × t    (joules or kWh)

1 kWh = 1 kW × 3600 s = 3,600,000 J (kilowatt-hour)

✏️ Worked Examples

Example 1

Electric heater: 240 V, 2.5 A. (a) Power rating? (b) Resistance?

(a) P = VI = 240×2.5 = 600 W

(b) R = V/I = 240/2.5 = 96 Ω

Example 2 — Cost

Kettle: 2500 W, 240 V, 6 hours. Cost at Ksh 1.10/kWh?

Energy = 2.5 kW × 6 h = 15 kWh

Cost = 15 × 1.10 = Ksh 16.50

Example 3 — Heater

R=100Ω, 240V mains. Find power rating and current.

P = V²/R = 240²/100 = 576 W | I = P/V = 576/240 = 2.4 A

🏠 Domestic Wiring System

  • Two cables: Live (L) = 240 V and Neutral (N) = 0 V (earthed)
  • Frequency: 50 Hz
  • Main fuse on live wire
  • Joule meter measures energy consumed (kWh)
  • Consumer box (fuse box) with circuit breakers
  • Ring main for sockets (starts and ends at consumer unit)
  • Three-pin plugs: Live, Neutral, Earth
⚠️ A fuse is a short wire that melts when current exceeds its rating — protecting the circuit.

🔍 Why Transmit at High Voltage?

Power loss in cables: Ploss = I²R

High voltage → low current (P = VI, same power). Lower current → much less heat loss in cables.

Ploss = I²R — reduce I to dramatically reduce loss
Transmitting at 400 kV instead of 25 kV reduces current by 16× and power loss by 256×!

🧪 Practice Problems

P1. An iron rated 1000W, 240V. Find: (a) current, (b) resistance, (c) cost for 30 min at Ksh 5/kWh.

(a) I=P/V=1000/240=4.17A | (b) R=V/I=57.6Ω | (c) Energy=1kW×0.5h=0.5kWh; Cost=0.5×5=Ksh 2.50

P2. Why is electricity transmitted at high voltage rather than low voltage?

High voltage means low current (P=VI). Low current means less power lost as heat in cables (P=I²R).
