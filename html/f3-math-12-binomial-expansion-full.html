Expand (x + 2)³:
= x³ + 3x²(2) + 3x(4) + 8 = x³ + 6x² + 12x + 8
Expand (2x − 1)⁴:
a=2x, b=−1
= (2x)⁴ + 4(2x)³(−1) + 6(2x)²(−1)² + 4(2x)(−1)³ + (−1)⁴
= 16x⁴ − 32x³ + 24x² − 8x + 1

Pascal's Triangle

Each number is the sum of the two numbers above it.

Rule: Each entry = sum of the two entries directly above it.
Row n gives the coefficients of (a+b)ⁿ.

Numerical Applications

Use binomial expansion to evaluate expressions like (1.02)⁴ or (0.98)³.

Evaluate (1.02)⁴:
Write as (1 + 0.02)⁴
= 1⁴ + 4(1³)(0.02) + 6(1²)(0.02²) + 4(1)(0.02³) + (0.02)⁴
= 1 + 0.08 + 0.0024 + 0.000032 + 0.00000016
≈ 1.08243216
Evaluate (0.99)³:
(1 − 0.01)³ = 1 − 3(0.01) + 3(0.0001) − (0.000001)
= 1 − 0.03 + 0.0003 − 0.000001 = 0.970299

General Term and Finding Specific Terms

General term T_(r+1) in (a+b)ⁿ:
T_(r+1) = C(n,r) × aⁿ⁻ʳ × bʳ
where C(n,r) = n! / (r!(n−r)!)
Find the 3rd term in (x+2)⁴:
r=2: T₃ = C(4,2) × x² × 2² = 6 × x² × 4 = 24x²
Find the term with x² in (2+x)⁴:
a=2, b=x; need bʳ = xʳ → r=2
T₃ = C(4,2) × 2² × x² = 6 × 4 × x² = 24x²

  • Sum of coefficients of (a+b)ⁿ = 2ⁿ

  • Coefficients are symmetric in Pascal's triangle

More Worked Examples

Expand (3x − 2y)³:
a=3x, b=−2y; coefficients: 1 3 3 1
= (3x)³ + 3(3x)²(−2y) + 3(3x)(−2y)² + (−2y)³
= 27x³ − 54x²y + 36xy² − 8y³
Find the constant term in (x + 1/x)⁴:
T_(r+1) = C(4,r) × xⁿ⁻ʳ × (1/x)ʳ = C(4,r) × x⁴⁻²ʳ
Constant when 4−2r=0 → r=2
T₃ = C(4,2) = 6
