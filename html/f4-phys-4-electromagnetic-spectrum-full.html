🌈 The Electromagnetic Spectrum

A continuum of all EM waves arranged by frequency and wavelength. Produced when electrically charged particles oscillate or change energy. Travel perpendicularly to both electric and magnetic fields.

📡 Common Properties of EM Waves

  • Do not require a medium — travel through vacuum
  • Undergo reflection, refraction and diffraction
  • All travel at c = 3×10⁸ m/s in vacuum
  • Carry no electric charge
  • Transfer energy as oscillating electric and magnetic fields
  • Obey the wave equation: v = fλ

Examples

VHF radio at 30 MHz: λ = v/f = 3×10⁸/30×10⁶ = 10 m

Radio wave at λ=150 m: f = v/λ = 3×10⁸/150 = 2 MHz

📻 Radio & Microwaves

Radio Waves

Subdivided into LW, MW, SW. Produced by oscillators. Easily diffracted by small objects (houses) but not large ones (hills). Used in radio, TV, military communications.

Microwaves

Produced by oscillating charges in dish aerials. Detected by RADAR (Radio Detection and Ranging). Used in: radar, speed guns, microwave ovens, telephone and data communication.

SLIDE 5 OF 10

🔥 Infrared, Visible & UV

Infrared (IR)

Close to microwaves = thermal (heat from sun, fire). Close to visible = no thermal (TV remote). Detected by skin, photographic film. Used in: satellite vegetation imaging, hospitals, burglar alarms, green houses.

Visible Light (ROYGBIV)

Tiny part of spectrum. Wavelengths visible to human eye. Used in photosynthesis, remote sensing, identification.

Ultraviolet (UV)

Emitted by hot objects (sun, welding). Can cause skin cancer and cataracts. Used in: detecting fake currency, making reflective materials.

SLIDE 6 OF 10

☢️ X-rays & Gamma Rays

X-rays

Very short wavelength, high energy. Produced in X-ray tubes (high-speed electrons stopped by metal). Detected by photographic film or fluorescent screen.

Uses: fractures, cancer radiotherapy, detecting cracks in metal, airport security.

Gamma Rays

Produced by radioactive materials (large energy changes in nuclei). Detected by photographic film, Geiger-Müller tube, cloud chamber.

Uses: sterilise instruments, kill weevils in grain, treat cancer, X-ray-like photography.

✏️ Worked Examples

Example 1

Calculate the wavelength of a 900 MHz microwave.

λ = c/f = 3×10⁸ / 900×10⁶ = 0.333 m = 33.3 cm

Example 2

An EM wave has wavelength 6×10⁻⁷ m. What colour is it and what is its frequency?

f = c/λ = 3×10⁸/6×10⁻⁷ = 5×10¹⁴ Hz — visible green/yellow light

Example 3

A gamma ray has energy E = hf where h = 6.63×10⁻³⁴ Js and f = 3×10²⁰ Hz. Find E.

E = 6.63×10⁻³⁴ × 3×10²⁰ = 1.99×10⁻¹³ J

🧪 Practice Problems

P1. Find the wavelength of a radio wave of frequency 600 kHz.

λ = 3×10⁸/600×10³ = 500 m

P2. State the EM wave with: (a) longest wavelength (b) highest energy per photon.

(a) Radio waves | (b) Gamma rays

P3. Why are X-rays dangerous to living cells?

High energy — can ionize atoms in cells, damaging DNA and causing mutations or cancer.
