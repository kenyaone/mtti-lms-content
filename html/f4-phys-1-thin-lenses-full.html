🔍 Types of Thin Lenses

A lens is a piece of glass or plastic used to focus or change the direction of a beam of light passing through it. Lenses are used in spectacles, cameras, microscopes and telescopes.

Converging (Convex) Lens

Thicker at the centre than at the edges. Converges (brings together) parallel rays of light to a real focus.

Diverging (Concave) Lens

Thicker at the edges than at the centre. Diverges (spreads out) parallel rays — virtual focus.

🏷️ Properties of Lenses

Key Terms

PropertyDefinition
Optical Centre (O)Geometric centre of the lens. Rays passing through O are undeviated.
Principal AxisImaginary line through O, perpendicular to the lens.
Principal Focus (F)Point where rays parallel to principal axis converge (convex) or appear to diverge from (concave) after refraction.
Focal Length (f)Distance from optical centre to principal focus.
Centre of CurvatureCentre of the sphere of which the lens surface is part. Denoted C and C₁.
⚠️ The principal focus for a converging lens is real; for a diverging lens it is virtual.
📌 Unlike mirrors, the principal focus is NOT halfway between the optical centre and centre of curvature.

📐 Images Formed by a Converging Lens

3 Important Rays for Construction

  1. Ray parallel to principal axis → refracts through F
  2. Ray through optical centre → passes straight through (undeviated)
  3. Ray through F → refracts parallel to principal axis

📏 The Lens Formula

1/f = 1/u + 1/v

u = object distance (positive for real object)
v = image distance (positive for real image, negative for virtual)
f = focal length (positive for converging, negative for diverging)

Linear Magnification

m = v/u = image height / object height

Power of a Lens

P = 1/f    (unit: Dioptre, D)

Converging lens → positive power. Diverging lens → negative power.

Worked Example

Q: An object is 12 cm from a converging lens of focal length 18 cm. Find the image position.

Solution: f = +18, u = +12

1/v = 1/f − 1/u = 1/18 − 1/12 = 2/36 − 3/36 = −1/36

∴ v = −36 cm → virtual, erect image behind the object.

🔭 Optical Devices Using Lenses

1. Simple Microscope (Magnifying Glass)

Object placed inside focal length → virtual, magnified, erect image at 25 cm (least distance of distinct vision). Shorter focal length = greater magnification.

2. Compound Microscope

Two lenses: objective lens (near object) + eyepiece lens (near eye). Used to observe micro-organisms, cells, Brownian motion.

3. Astronomical Telescope

Two lenses: objective (large f) + eyepiece (small f). Used to view distant objects like stars.

4. Camera

Converging lens system + shutter + diaphragm (aperture) + film/sensor in a light-proof box.

5. Human Eye

Cornea + aqueous humour + crystalline lens = converging system. Ciliary muscles change lens curvature (accommodation). Image on retina is inverted but brain corrects it.

✏️ More Worked Examples

Example 1 — Magnification

A converging lens (f = 10 cm) forms an image 5× magnified on a screen. Find object distance.

m = v/u = 5 → v = 5u

1/f = 1/u + 1/v → 1/10 = 1/u + 1/5u = 6/5u → u = 12 cm

Example 2 — Power

Find the power of a concave lens of focal length 25 cm.

f = −0.25 m (negative for concave)

P = 1/f = 1/(−0.25) = −4 D

Example 3 — Slide Projector

Image height 1.5 m on screen 9.0 m away. Slide picture height 6.5 cm. Find (a) object distance (b) focal length.

m = 150/6.5 = 900/u → u = 39 cm

1/f = 1/39 + 1/900 → f ≈ 37.4 cm

SLIDE 9 OF 10

🧪 Practice Problems

Problem 1

A converging lens has focal length 15 cm. An object is placed 30 cm from the lens. Find: (a) image distance, (b) magnification, (c) state the nature of the image.

Solution:
1/v = 1/15 − 1/30 = 2/30 − 1/30 = 1/30
v = 30 cm (real, same side as image)
m = 30/30 = 1 (same size, inverted, real)

Problem 2

A diverging lens has focal length 20 cm. An object is 40 cm away. Find the image position and magnification.

Solution:
f = −20 cm, u = +40 cm
1/v = 1/(−20) − 1/40 = −2/40 − 1/40 = −3/40
v = −13.3 cm (virtual, same side as object)
m = 13.3/40 = 0.33 (diminished, erect)
